How To Play:

Introduction

The "Game of Life" is a zero-player game, meaning that once the initial input is received (i.e. the seed),
the game begins and continues for an infinite amount of time without requiring input from the player.
In this way, it's more closely related to a simulation rather than a game.


Rules

The playing board representing the game universe consists of a grid of cells, each of which can interact with its
8 neighboring cells and exists in one of two states: alive or dead. Generations are based on the state
of the previous universe. During the creation of each successive generation, individual cells follow this protocol:


-> Any cell that is alive and has 2-3 neighbors that are also alive survives to the next generation

-> Any cell that is dead and has exactly 3 neighbors that are alive becomes alive in the next generation

-> Any other cell that is alive becomes dead; likewise, any other cell that is dead remains dead


The Seed

This program contains several seed presets to choose from as well as a blank preset. For the blank preset, the player
can choose individual cells to populate. After populating the playing board, the player can press "Start" to generate
successive generations on a timed interval. Once the board no longer contains any populated (alive) cells, the
game is essentially over.
